We’re looking forward to having you. This page has everything you need to plan your visit — how to get here, what to bring, and a few things we’d like to know in advance.

Getting Here

Invercharron House, near Ardgay, Sutherland IV24 3DN — Get directions

By Air

Fly to Inverness Airport (INV). There are regular flights from London, Edinburgh, Bristol, Manchester, and other UK cities.

Flight to Inverness ~1.5 hrs from London
Drive to Invercharron ~50 mins, 40 miles via A9 north

From Inverness Airport you can hire a car (Hertz, Enterprise, and Avis all have desks at the airport) or book a taxi:

Ronnie’s Taxis
Tel: 01863 766422
Mobile: 0777 818 1208

The drive takes you north on the A9 across the Cromarty Firth, through Tain, over the Dornoch Firth Bridge, and on to Ardgay. A good road.

By Train

LNER runs direct trains from London King’s Cross to Inverness via Edinburgh and the Highland Main Line through Perth and Aviemore. A good journey.

London King’s Cross → Inverness ~7.5–8.5 hrs direct
Taxi or drive to Invercharron ~50 mins from Inverness

Book at lner.co.uk or thetrainline.com. You can also change at Edinburgh and take a ScotRail service onward — the Edinburgh to Inverness leg (~3.5 hrs) passes through the Cairngorms and is a lovely route.

From Inverness, the same taxi or hire car options apply as above.

By Sleeper Train

The Caledonian Sleeper runs overnight from London Euston to Inverness.

Depart London Euston ~21:00
Arrive Inverness ~08:30 next morning
Taxi or drive to Invercharron ~50 mins

Choose from seated, classic rooms (bunk beds) or club rooms (double bed with en suite). A lounge car serves supper and breakfast. The service runs Sunday to Friday nights.

Book at caledoniansleeper.com — club rooms sell out quickly, so book early.

What to Pack

Evenings

Smart casual unless otherwise instructed — jacket, smart trousers / smart dress, and smart shoes.

Northern Meeting

White tie for the summer meeting; black tie for the winter meeting.

Daytime

Warm, comfortable clothes. The Highlands can be brisk even in summer — layers are your friend.

Waterproofs

A waterproof coat is strongly advised. It will rain.

Wellies

Wellington boots are available at the house, but if it’s a large party you may not get your size — consider bringing your own.

Swimming

In summer, bring swimming gear. The river is cold but good.
